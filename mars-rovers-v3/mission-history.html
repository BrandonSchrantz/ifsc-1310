NASA Mars Rovers

What a wonder the reaches of space are. Exspecially the red planet, Mars. Many people have often wondered what sights that planet has to present, but for the longest time, our eyes could only get as close to it through a telescopic lense. But curiosity of this world, one more similar to Earth than any other planet in the solar system, would not be stymied by a little distance of 140 million miles. So we launched unmanned rovers, and they actually made there! But what did these rovers find and discoveries they make? What technologies did they employ? And what exactly was their target objective?

Meet the Rovers

While there are a number of unmaned robotics that have been sent to mars, in total there have been five NASA rovers that have landed on this "new" strange world that has only recently been brought within reach of humanity.

  • Spirit
  • Opportunity
  • Curiosity
  • Perseverance
  • Sojourner

The First Three

While we could talk forever about all the different spacecraftes that have been sent to Mars, or at least attempted, the first of the three NASA rovers to navigate 'the red rock' is Sojourner, and the Spirit and Opportunity. The Sojourner (pictured in the top left) was the frist to touch Mars's surface when it landed on July 4, 1997. It laid the groundwork and proof on concept for the following rovers, such as, Spririt and Opportunity (top right) which launched within a small window of each other in the summer of 2003. Opportunities mission status did not end until 2019 remarkable enough.

The Ones Still Going Strong

But what about the rovers still active on Mars today? We are of course talking about the latest of the five to get there successfully, Curiosity and Perseverance. These two have the benefit and thanks of the first three rovers in expreience. There mission status is active and have overlapping objectives.
