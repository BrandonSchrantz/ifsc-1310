Mission History of Curiosity and Perseverance

The NASA Mars rovers "Curiosity" and "Perseverance" are the only two NASA rovers currently active on Mars and continuing the ongoing search for knowledge. This fact is certainly memorialized in thier very names. Curiosity is after all, what drives mankind to reach for the stars. It is a simple drive to learn more. But obtaining knowledge is not without a great deal of Perseverance. Perseverance that has withstanded decades of hard trial and error.

Mission Goals

Curiosity Determine if Mars was habitable to microbial life in the past. (Remeber: Mars is the most Earth-like planet in the solar system.)
Perseverance Similar to Curiosity, find evidence of microbial life that could have been present in the past. Plus, collect rock and soil samples for potential future retrieval mission.

Launch and Landing

Curiosity Curiosity launched from Cape Canaveral on November 26, 2011. After 254 days in space, Curiosity landed in the Gale Crater on August 6, 2012.
Perseverance Perseverance launched from Cape Canaveral on July 20, 2020. After 203 days in space, Perseverance landed in the Jezero Crater on February 18, 2021.

Design Features

Curiosity Curiosity was the biggest Mars Rover Nasa had land at the time. It has three types of cameras. Four types of spectrometers. Two types of radiation detectors. Environmental and atmospheric sensors. And a robotic arm with a drill.
Perseverance Similar to Curiosity, but: robotic arm differs. There is a larger "hand" and the scientific instruments are different. This rover specializes in sample retrieval and its body is suited to that, including new motars. And as seen in the image to the left, the wheels have been modified.

Accomplishments

Curiosity

It didn't take long for Curiosity to make its first big discovery. Located in the Gale Crater, there existed mineral and chemical evidence that of a habitable environment. The Gale Crater was at one point apairently home to shallow rivers and lakes. Also, found in Yellowknife Bay, were elements that are necessary for life, giving rise to further possibilities. Additionally discoveries of organic compounds, atmospheric methane, and a deduction of radiation amounts were made. Curiosity continues its ongoing mission.

Perseverance

Various discoveries related to the volcanic, habitabilty, presence of water, and history have been made by Perseverance as it relates to its landing location in the Jezero Crater. Firstly volcanic rocks were found. This discover occured within one year of its mission. Also found were anciemt lakebeds, which were sampled. The role of water on Mars has been made clearer. Perseverance also managed to turn carbon-dioxide into oxygen. Lastly, sound was recorded.
